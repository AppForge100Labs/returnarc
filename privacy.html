Privacy

Returnarc is designed to keep habit information on your device. AppForge100 does not collect data from the app.

Effective date: 16 July 2026

Information you create

Habit names, schedules, reminders, completion history, skips, pauses, appearance choices and recovery progress are stored locally on your device. The app has no AppForge100 account, backend or cloud-sync service.

Apple device backup

The protected app database may participate in standard Apple device backup when your Apple and device settings allow it. Apple controls backup storage and restoration. Temporary exports, widget projections and app commands are not the authoritative database.

Apple platform services

The app uses Apple frameworks for local notifications, widgets, Siri and Shortcuts, and App Store purchases. Apple may process information required to provide those services under Apple’s terms and privacy policy. AppForge100 does not receive habit content through them.

No advertising or tracking

The app contains no advertising SDK, cross-app tracking, third-party product SDK or remote analytics transport. AppForge100 does not sell habit information or use it for targeted advertising.

Export and deletion

You can export your habit data from Settings. You can also delete all local habit data from Settings. Deleting the app removes its local container subject to any backup retained under your Apple settings.

Security

The authoritative database uses Apple platform file protection and remains app-private. No system can be guaranteed perfectly secure, so keep your device and Apple account protected and do not place highly sensitive information in habit titles.

Children

The app is a general habit tool and is not directed to children. It does not knowingly collect personal information from children because AppForge100 does not collect app data.

Changes

If the app’s data practices change, this notice and the App Store privacy disclosure will be updated before the change takes effect where required.
